Our History

In 1892, the City of Norfolk purchased the land that this Zoo is currently on.
In 1900, it started becoming a zoo through purchasing 200+ animals.
In 1974, we separated from the city park and renovated a lot of our exhibits.
In 1992, we renovated a lot of our exhibits and got a lot of new parts!

Our Animals

We have plenty of gardens and exhibits for every animal here at The Virginia Zoo!

  1. North America
    North America’s finest noble animals can be found in this area of the Zoo.
  2. Africa-Okavango Delta
    Venture to Africa-Okavango Delta, home to a spectacular collection of scenic landscapes and impressive creatures, big and small.
  3. Zoo Farm
    The Farm has the biggest variety of animals at the Zoo. Farm animals, as well as exotic creatures from all over the world can be found here.
  4. World of Reptiles
    Slither into the World of Reptiles and Friends to meet animals big and small, scaly slimy and even furry.
  5. Asia - Trail of the Tiger
    Cascading waterfalls, overlooking views of scenic exhibits, vibrant foliage and a vast array of exotic animals hailing from dozens of Asian countries are what make up Asia – Trail of the Tiger.
  6. Animal Ambassadors
    The program animal collection is distinct from the large collection of animals on exhibit at the Virginia Zoo, which are not used in programs (with the exception of some of those in the Zoo Farm).
  7. Animal Wellness Camp
    The Virginia Zoo’s Animal Wellness Campus is a comprehensive, state-of- the-art medical and diet facility that serves the more than 500 animals that call the Zoo their home.
